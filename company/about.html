We're just three kids

Three kids in Canada who met and created countless ideas. Now those ideas are finally coming to life.

The beginning

Strova started with three teenagers bouncing ideas in a Discord server. Two of us in Canada, one currently in the USA. We were all animators first, dreamers second. And we were all broke.

We'd pirated industry software, paid for subscriptions we couldn't afford, and watched companies buy each other until there were only two options left: Adobe's monopoly or learning Blender and praying.

Animation tools shouldn't require a corporate budget. The best ideas come from bedrooms, not boardrooms.

Who we are

We're not a startup. Not a company. Just three friends with complementary skills who got tired of waiting for someone else to build what we needed.

Why we built this

Corporate animation software has a pattern: get users hooked, raise prices, add AI features nobody asked for, ignore bugs for years. We watched subscription costs triple while innovation stalled.

So we started building. Not a startup. Not a product for acquisition. Just software we'd actually want to use—fast, focused, and fair.

Flow technology came from our own frustration. We were manually keyframing the same bounces and arcs hundreds of times. There had to be a faster way. Now there is.

Our stance

Strova will never take venture capital. We won't sell to a bigger company. We won't add AI features that replace artists. We're not building an "ecosystem" to trap you in.

We're building the tool we wished existed. If it helps you make something cool, that's enough.

Software doesn't need to be a subscription service. It can just be software.

What's next

We're not trying to replace industry giants. We're proving that three teenagers with laptops can build something honest in a field full of exploitation.

Thanks for caring enough to read this. Try Strova. Break it. Tell us what sucks. We'll fix it.
